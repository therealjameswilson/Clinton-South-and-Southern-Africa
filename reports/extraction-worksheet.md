## Universal Extraction Fields

- Document boundary: exact PDF file and page range for the record.
- Heading: document type, title, participants, and location if applicable.
- Date line: document date, Washington time, and any transmission date.
- Markings: original classification, handling markings, precedence, channel, and declassification stamp.
- Draft/clear/approve: drafter, clearance, approving official, and distribution/read-by list.
- Omitted material: withheld pages, excisions, withdrawal sheets, and cited exemptions.
- Selection rationale: why the document shows a major policy decision, significant diplomatic activity, or essential context.
- Annotation hooks: people, organizations, events, related records, and explanatory footnotes needed.
- Source note: final FRUS-style repository path, item ID/case number/NAID, file title, markings, and page span.
- Disposition: `Include`, `Context`, `Drop`, or `Replacement search`.

## Packet-Splitting Rule

Do not cite a multi-document PDF packet as a single final FRUS document unless the packet itself is the record being documented. For FOIA and MDR packets, create a separate extraction note for each released document that could stand as a FRUS selection, then attach the withdrawal sheets and omitted-material accounting to the relevant source note.
